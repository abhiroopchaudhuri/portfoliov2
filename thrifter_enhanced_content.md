## 1 · Executive Summary
**Thrifter: a verified, editorial marketplace for pre-loved luxury fashion.**
Thrifter disrupts the peer-to-peer thrift market by wrapping the thrill of luxury thrifting inside the operational rigor of modern e-commerce — authentication, standardized condition grading, logistics, and returns.

**Role framing (interpretive, additive):**
- Role: Lead Product Designer — Research, UX, UI, IA
- Timeline: 8 weeks (discovery to high-fidelity + AI validation)
- Platform: Responsive Web + Mobile Web

---

## 2 · Reframing the Problem
The original case lists three pain points. At senior level, they compound into a single, sharper thesis:

> **Luxury thrifting lives in a trust vacuum.** Buyers cannot verify authenticity, sellers cannot predict sale timing, and there is no neutral intermediary enforcing price, condition, or returns. The category does not need a new catalog — it needs a new contract.

This reframing is used to prioritize every downstream trade-off (e.g., "verified-first" nav, condition-tier filtering, seller-side operations in the roadmap).

## 3 · Business & User Goals
Written from the original "The Goal" paragraph, structured for a product review.

**User goals**
- Access authenticated luxury and high-street brands at student- and young-professional-friendly prices.
- Shop with confidence via standardized condition grading and buyer protection.
- A modern, e-commerce-native experience with advanced filtering (size, condition, era, brand).

**Business goals (interpretive)**
- Establish a neutral intermediary that captures share from fragmented Instagram/P2P sellers.
- Unlock high user retention through a risk-free purchasing loop.
- Enable a sustainability narrative without pricing it as the primary hook (research shows it is not).

## 4 · Research Method & Rigor
A genuinely mixed-methods study, which is treated in the page as three stacked sections.

**Qualitative — 45 interviews (original figure)**
- Semi-structured interviews around a 15-question protocol (all 15 preserved verbatim on the live page).
- Recruited across age, income, profession, gender, and geography — including traditionalists who rarely shop online.

**Quantitative — 10-question survey (original Yes/No data)**
- All ten original questions and percentages preserved and presented with dual-bar visual comparisons.
- Headline reframing (not new data, just better surfaced):
  - 82% want luxury thrift at lower prices → demand is the least of the risks.
  - 64% have already bought fakes → trust is the single biggest gating factor.
  - 76% are comfortable wearing slightly used high-end garments → the stigma is fading.
  - 60% want virtual try-on → clear roadmap signal for AR.
  - 16% shop primarily for sustainability → sustainability is a feel-good layer, not the hook.

**Competitive — 10 platforms audited (original list preserved)**
- Direct resellers: ThredUp, Amalfi, Thrifted, Rethought, Thrift+
- Adjacent: Etsy
- Benchmarks: Shoppers Stop, Nykaa Fashion, Amazon, Flipkart
- Heuristic finding: existing thrift platforms read as visually "cheap". To sell luxury, the UI itself has to read as luxury.

**Proprietary data (original NDA data preserved as three "cuts")**
1. Popular brands among luxury-brand shoppers → feeds merchandising & featured collections.
2. Average discount percentage by age group → informs tiered pricing strategy.
3. Brands with discount percentage > 25% → sets the default "Deals" cut-off and email triggers.

## 5 · Synthesis — Themes (additive framing, built from original data)
Four themes distilled from the interview set. None invents new user claims; they cluster what is already in the original transcripts and empathy map.

1. **The "Wild West" Anxiety** — Instagram thrift stores feel like a gamble (no returns, hidden damage, whimsical pricing).
2. **Paradox of Choice** — appetite for unique items, but decision fatigue without condition tiers, strong filters and categorization.
3. **Sustainability as a Bonus** — only 16% convert on sustainability; the real trigger is accessible luxury.
4. **Trust is Earned Through Proof** — authenticity badges, real condition photography, and published returns policy are table stakes.
